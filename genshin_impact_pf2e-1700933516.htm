Welcome to Teyvat

You have arrived in Teyvat — a fantasy world where the seven elements flow and converge.
In the distant past, the Archons granted mortals unique elemental abilities. With the help of such powers, people formed a bountiful homeland out of the wilderness. However, 500 years ago, the collapse of an ancient civilization turned the universe upside down…
Though the calamity the world suffered has ceased, peace has yet to be restored.

What is this?

This is a fan-made, unofficial supplement for Pathfinder 2e that adds various items, enemies, and mechanics from the world of Teyvat, the main setting for Genshin Impact. Elemental combat is added to party, enemy and environmental interactions, making combat even more strategic and teamwork oriented.

The seven elements are a core part of the worldbuilding and mechanics of the setting, consisting of Anemo, Geo, Electro, Dendro, Hydro, Pyro, and Cryo. Each element interacts with other elements in unique ways that can trigger certain Elemental Reactions, which can provide additional damage, utility, and more.

The next section will be about Teyvat as a setting and how to work around it with PF2e rules and a general setting summary for those new to Genshin Impact's colorful world.

Teyvat as a Setting

Ancestries

While the majority of playable characters in Teyvat are human, there is a wide and colorful range of peoples and types of creatures that can serve as playable ancestries and characters. Existing nations and ingame cultures draw inspiration from and can be considered the fantasy equivalent versions of several real life countries and cultures, in a similar way to Golarion.
    Starting with the classic fantasy staples, elves exist in Teyvat but appear to be extremely rare, but dwarves don't as of yet, despite many playable characters or important historical lore figures being references to dwarves in Norse mythology. Thus, a bit of reflavoring in either direction can do the trick here. Ancestries like gnomes, orcs, and halflings don't really have any existing equivalents.
    Besides the fantasy classics, there are also characters that appear human but with animal features, such as cat ears or a tail. Ancestries like these can easily be used as reflavored catfolk, shoony, or beastkin. In general, the more 'anime' an ancestry is the easier it can be fitted into existing canon, but this game takes so much inspiration from so many different cultures and mythos that nearly anything can work with enough reflavoring.
    In addition, there are also more mythical playable creatures, such as Japanese youkai like the kitsune, oni, or tengu, illuminated beasts or adepti from Chinese mythology such as qilin or xiezhi, yakshas from Buddhist or Hindu texts, homonculi crafted from alchemy, advanced automatons, puppets, or other artificially created beings that appear mostly indistinguishable from humans, and so much more.

Visions

A Vision is an item bestowed by the gods to people on Teyvat. It grants the user the ability to directly control a single given element matching that of their Vision. Visions take the form of indestructible glowing gems with the respective element's symbol in the center, encased by a decorative design corresponding to one of the seven nations.
    Without a Vision, it is thought to be impossible to directly control the elements in the way that elemental creatures or gods innately can, and controlling multiple elements is practically unheard of. Given the fact that Vision wielders are extremely rare in Teyvat, making up a very small minority of the population, and the criteria for obtaining a Vision are unknown, many people believe Vision wielders to be chosen or blessed by the gods. However, contrary to popular belief in Teyvat, it is not by the direct will of the Archons that Visions are granted. The key to gaining one seems to be related to one's ambition. It is said that when a person's ambition reaches it's peak, a Vision manifests before them.

Classes

Given the above information about visions, an obvious contender for a Vision wielding class comes to mind: the kineticist! In many ways, a Vision can be likened to the internal kinetic gate through which kineticists channel their elements. When running a campaign in Teyvat, consider granting free archetype to the players to select kineticist dedication, or even running a full party of kineticists with free archetype to allow further specialization. This serves as a great way to have the entire party be able to interact with the mechanics of Elemental Reactions, and synergize with each other, whether they be playing as vision wielders, mystical creatures, or other kinds of characters who have the ability to wield the elements.
    Besides kineticist, there are also many other professions throughout Teyvat that can map onto existing classes. Alchemy in particular plays a very important key role and central theme throughout the worldbuilding and story of Genshin Impact. As such, alchemist makes an excellent choice for a class, especially with its ability to create all kinds of bombs to trigger Elemental Reactions on the fly. Bards, fighters, investigators, rangers, thaumaturges, and even uncommon classes such as gunslingers and inventors all exist in Teyvat in one form or another.
    Even wizards and other spellcasters could work well in Teyvat, despite Visions being the primary way through which humans obtain magical abilities with the elements. As such, the Elementalist class archetype is particularly appropriate for these classes. Consider granting other spellcasting classes access to every spell matching the trait of their Vision's element. However, when playing classes whose magical ability is more innate in nature, such as sorcerer, playable ancestries who are more innately magical than humans may be more appropriate, such as Inazuma's youkai or the adepti of Liyue. Nations

Just as the elements of Teyvat number seven, so too do the nations, each corresponding to an element and under the rule of one of The Seven Archons. Archons are deities who represent the pinnacle of each element. They are victors of the continent-spanning conflict known as the Archon War that ended 2000 years ago, a time where countless gods fought and killed each other for ownership of one of the seven divine seats that had opened in Celestia, and with it, the right to rule a nation through a divine Gnosis. Each Archon has an ideal, and this ideal manifests itself in the way each nation is portrayed, their government, the surrounding geography, and their culture.

Mondstadt

A city of freedom that lies in the northeast of Teyvat. From amongst mountains and wide-open plains, carefree breezes carry the scent of dandelions — a gift from the Anemo God, Barbatos — across Cider Lake to Mondstadt, which sits on an island in the middle of the lake.
    Mondstadt is the nation of Anemo, "ruled" by the Anemo Archon, Barbatos. Barbatos' ideal is Freedom, and as a result, he doesn't like the idea of ruling his nation directly, preferring to let his people have the freedom to make their own choices. Despite this, the absentee Archon has a devoted following of adherents in the Church of Favonius, a religious organization managed by the Knights of Favonius, who serve as one of the Four Winds that protect the idyllic and peaceful nation. Ever since the dissolution of the old aristocracy and fall of the Lawrence clan 1000 years ago, there has been little socioeconomic stratification among the citizens of Mondstadt. Thus, the carefree people of Mondstadt have been able to enjoy leisurely days spent drinking dandelion wine and annual festivities such as Ludi Harpastum and the Windblume Festival.
    Mondstadt is based on and takes cues and inspiration from European cultures, mostly German. It's geography mostly consists of lush, windswept hills, valleys, and highlands, interspersed with forests and cliffs overlooking the ocean to the east.

Liyue

A bountiful harbor that lies in the east of Teyvat. Mountains stand tall and proud alongside the stone forest, that, together with the open plains and lively rivers, make up Liyue's bountiful landscape, which shows its unique beauty through each of the four seasons. Just how many gifts from the Geo God lie in wait amongst the rocks of Liyue's mountains?
    Liyue is the nation of Geo, ruled by the Geo Archon Morax, also known as Rex Lapis. Morax's ideal is Contracts. He is the oldest Archon among The Seven, and one of the original victors of the Archon War along with Barbatos. He has personally walked alongside his nation throughout all of history, descending every year to personally administrate his nation via divine predictions during the Rite of Descension. As a result, Liyue has maintained a strong focus on business and trade, and even the main currency of Teyvat, Mora, is solely minted in Liyue and named after it's god. Liyue Harbor is the largest and most prosperous commercial port in all of Teyvat, and the largest city in the nation of Liyue.

    Liyue is based on and takes cues and inspiration from China, especially ancient Chinese myth. Adepti, also known as Xian in Chinese Daoism, are mythical creatures ranging from illuminated beasts to gods, and many have signed contracts during the Archon War to serve under Morax and protect Liyue to this day. The nations geography mostly consists of mountains, karsts in which the adepti are said to live, stone forests, and flooded plains.

Inazuma

An Isolated Archipelago Far East of Teyvat. Overcome endless thunderstorms and set foot on the islands of red maple and cherry blossoms. On winding shores and towering cliffs, and in forests and mountains full of secrets, witness the Eternity pursued by Her Excellency, the Almighty Narukami Ogosho.
    Inazuma is the nation of Electro, ruled by the Electro Archon Baal, also known as the Raiden Shogun. Baal's ideal is Eternity. She rules her nation with an iron fist, and within the past year of the game relative to current events at the start, Inazuma has made a drastic turn towards isolationism, locked down under the Sakoku Decree. Baal also enacted the related Vision Hunt Decree, declaring that Visions, as gifts from the gods, should be in the sole hands of divinity — and is now forcefully seizing all Visions in Inazuma to inlay them in the hands of a statue of the Thousand-Armed, Hundred-Eyed God. On the other side of the archipelago, the residents of Watatsumi Island have formed a resistance to fight against this decree from Narukami Island, and thus even more bubbling turmoil blots the skies of Inazuma as this civil war continues to reach its boiling point.
    Inazuma is based on and takes cues and inspiration from ancient feudal Japan and Japanese folklore and myth. Many Japanese youkai are present and while some attempt to fit in and coexist with humans, others live in isolation in the wild. The nations geography mostly consists of various islands, similar to Japan.

Sumeru

The city of scholars located in the west-central part of Teyvat. A fantastical nation of both lush rainforest and barren desert, where countless fruits of wisdom grow and are buried. Whether Travelers travel from afar through the forest to reach the academy city or delve deep into the desert to discover the historical ruins of the red desert, a wealth of valuable knowledge awaits them here.
    Sumeru is the nation of Dendro, and while it has an Archon, known as Lesser Lord Kusanali, the primary rulers of Sumeru are in fact the people, specifically the sages of the Sumeru Akademiya. Lesser Lord Kusanali is the God of Wisdom, but 500 years ago during the Cataclysm the former Dendro Archon, Greater Lord Rukkhadevata, was slain. After her death, Lesser Lord Kusanali took her place. The Lesser Lord proved to be unable to provide the kind of 'wisdom' the sages and scholars of Sumeru sought and used to receive from the Greater Lord, and so they locked her up in the Sanctuary of Surasthana, which has served as her residence ever since. No one apart from the sages of the Akademiya has ever seen this deity with their own eyes for the last few hundred years. Under the aegis of the former God of Wisdom, the sages and scholars of Sumeru drive themselves into hysterics and abandon all that is worldly in their pursuit of esoteric wisdom.

A terrestrial sea in the center of Teyvat. Following the direction of pure currents, crossing wilderness, the depths of the forests and vastness of the sea of sand, arriving at the origin of all the waters of the continent. At the top of the waterfall, in the depths of the capital atop the terrestrial sea… a story that has never been heard, a legend that has been forgotten, like a lost kingdom sunken beneath the waves, yearning for a bard to sing its drowned songs.
    Fontaine is the nation of Hydro, ruled by the Hydro Archon Focalors, also known as Lady Furina. Focalor's ideal is Justice. As the God of Justice, she lives for the spectacle of the courtroom, and seeks to judge all other gods. She has a very unique and eccentric personality that's pervaded by a slight sense of impostor syndrome and anxiety. She acts in a very haughty and overly-theatrical manner, much to the chagrin of the nation's well-respected and extremely serious Chief Justice, Monsieur Neuvillette. She greatly enjoys having a form of presence in the trials of her nation, and the people of Fontaine treat her as more of a type of celebrity or even a mascot than with the reverence most would expect for a god.

Instead, the other unique energy source used in the city of Fontaine for daily needs such as lighting and travel is known as Indemnitium. It is generated from the process of judgement during trials, facilitated by a divinely constructed machine known as the Oratrice Mecanique d'Analyse Cardinale that harvests the people's collective belief in and excitement for the justice system and converts it into energy.

At the border edges of Fontaine are massive waterfalls that stretches for thousands of meters. Various water-based mythical creatures such as Melusines and Oceanids originate in Fontaine, although the Oceanids seem to abhor the current Hydro Archon, whereas Melusines are happy to serve as constables, guides, and other public servants in the nation.

The Tsaritsa's ideal is currently unknown, but it's speculated that at one point in time it was Love before possibly changing some time after the Cataclysm. The Seven Elements

There are seven main elements in the world of Teyvat. Most elements do not do anything on their own when applied to a target, but each one can trigger what is known as an Elemental Reaction when coming into contact with another element. The type of Elemental Reaction that is triggered depends on what elements are involved, and sometimes, the order in which they are applied.

List of Elements

Anemo

Anemo is the element of air and wind. Effects and creatures with the Air trait gain the Anemo trait. The corresponding status condition for afflicted creatures is called Anemo. Damaging effects with the Air trait deal Anemo damage.

Cryo

Cryo is the element of ice and snow. Effects and creatures with the Cold trait gain the Cryo trait. The corresponding status condition for afflicted creatures is called Cryo. Creatures afflicted with Cryo take a -5 status penalty to their speeds. Creatures with Cryo trait are immune to this penalty. Cold damage is synonymous with Cryo damage.

Dendro

Dendro is the element of plants and greenery. Effects and creatures with the Plant or Wood trait gain the Dendro trait. The corresponding status condition for afflicted creatures is called Dendro. Damaging effects with the Plant or Wood trait deal Dendro damage.

Electro

Electro is the element of electricity. Effects and creatures with the Electricity trait gain the Electro trait. The corresponding status condition for afflicted creatures is called Electro. Electricity damage is synonymous with Electro damage.

Geo

Geo is the element of earth and stone. Effects and creatures with the Earth trait gain the Geo trait. The corresponding elemental status condition for afflicted creatures is called Geo. Damaging effects with the Earth trait deal Geo damage.

Hydro

Hydro is the element of water. Effects and creatures with the Water trait gain the Hydro trait. The corresponding status condition for afflicted creatures is called Wet. Damaging effects with the Water trait deal Hydro damage.

Pyro

Pyro is the element of fire. Effects and creatures with the Fire trait gain the Pyro trait. The corresponding status condition for afflicted creatures is called Pyro. Fire damage is synonymous with Pyro damage.

Elemental Application

Whenever an effect with one of the seven elemental traits or damage types affects, damages, or otherwise makes contact with a creature or object, that creature or object is inflicted with the element's corresponding status condition. A creature or object inflicted with an elemental status condition can trigger Elemental Reactions when another elemental status condition is applied to it. Damage type takes priority over traits when determining what element is applied to a creature. For example, a spell with both the air and electricity traits that deals electricity damage applies Electro to its targets.
Whenever an element is applied to a creature or object, the status condition lasts for 2 rounds, except in the case of Anemo or Geo, which only last for the duration of the action or actions used to apply them. This is enough to trigger Anemo and Geo related Elemental Reactions.
Whenever an Elemental Reaction is triggered, the elements are typically consumed in the reaction and leave the target without an elemental status condition. Permanent elemental status conditions, such as those of certain elemental creatures, are never consumed when reacted with. Instead only the non-permanent triggering element is consumed when the Elemental Reaction occurs.

Elemental Application from Reactions

As a general rule, Elemental Reactions do not apply the elemental status condition of the type matching their damage type. For example, Overloaded does AoE Pyro damage, but does not apply Pyro to affected creatures. Likewise, Vaporize can amplify the amount of Hydro or Pyro damage an attack or effect deals, but this additional damage does not apply the respective element to the target. A notable exception to this is Swirl, whose sole function as an Elemental Reaction is to spread elements to nearby enemies.

Elements in the Environment

The world of Teyvat is built upon the foundations of the elements. Several things that are commonplace bear the elements within them.
    Any body of water such as the ocean, a flowing river, and even rain, applies the Wet status condition to creatures or objects that make contact with them. A creature becomes permanently Wet for as long as they stay in contact with such sources of water. A creature that ceases making contact with these sources of water loses the Wet condition after two rounds, like normal.
    Electro effects making contact with a body of water leave it Electro-Charged. Treat inanimate bodies of water as having an infinite amount of Hit Points, and resolve the persistent damage from Electro-Charged in all the affected squares as you would for a single creature. Cryo effects also leave affected squares of water Frozen for two rounds, providing solid ground to walk on.
    Open flames bear the element of Pyro, and thus apply Pyro to creatures or objects that would enter the flame. An Electro effect that passes through the flame, such as a Lightning Bolt spell, would trigger the appropriate Overloaded reaction in all squares that overlap with the flame.
    Grass, destructible wooden objects, and other foliage all bear the element of Dendro. However, as these are merely inert objects that do not apply effects, touching grass does not apply Dendro to creatures. These objects are only susceptible to Burning reactions. Treat Burning grass similarly to water in the above example, becoming charred and unable to burn again once the persistent damage is removed.

Elemental Reactions

Elemental Mastery

When an Elemental Reaction is triggered, the damage or strength of the effect usually scales with the amount of Elemental Mastery the triggering source has. Every creature has a certain amount of Elemental Mastery, including NPCs and monsters.
    At level 1 or lower, a creature has 0 points of Elemental Mastery, dealing only the base amount of damage dice for most Elemental Reactions. At 5th level, and every five levels thereafter, that creature gains 1 point of Elemental Mastery, for a total of 5 at level 25. In most cases, a creature's Elemental Reactions will deal one additional die of damage per point of Elemental Mastery the creature has.
    Elemental Reactions triggered by the environment rather than a creature usually only deal the base amount of damage dice, though the GM may determine otherwise for certain leveled environmental hazards when appropriate.

Harmless Elemental Reactions

Sometimes, a situation may occur where an Elemental Reaction would trigger due to an allies attempts to provide support or aid, or other innocuous situations like stepping into water. For example, an ally is afflicted with Pyro after taking damage, and another ally tries to heal them with a Hydro effect. As the Hydro effect is not a damaging effect, a harmless Vaporize reaction that deals no damage is triggered, rather than counterproductively Vaporizing the ally and dealing damage.
In general, self-inflicted applications of the elements and non-damaging effects trigger harmless Elemental reactions that do not damage the target, but otherwise still have their normal effects, if any (one could self-inflict a Swirl reaction to spread elements onto and damage nearby enemies, for example). The exceptions to this are Elemental Reactions that cause persistent damage rather than direct damage such as Electro-Charged and Burning. The persistent damage will still occur even when the Elemental Reaction is self-inflicted or from a non-damaging effect.

List of Elemental Reactions

Bloom

Bloom is triggered when Dendro and Hydro are applied to a target. Bloom spawns a tiny Dendro Core in a square of the targets space closest to the source of the reaction, and can be targetted separately and affected by Pyro and Electro effects.

Burning

Burning is triggered when Pyro and Dendro are applied to a target.
The target takes 1d4 persistent Pyro damage, and is considered Burning while the persistent damage is in effect. All creatures that grab, unarmed attack, or otherwise make direct physical contact with a Burning target immediately take this damage as well. The Burning status condition functions as the Pyro status condition, and can trigger the same Elemental Reactions that Pyro is associated with.
If the Burning status condition is removed via Elemental Reactions that would consume the status, the persistent Pyro damage ends immediately. If the target succeeds at the flat check to remove the persistent damage, or otherwise has the persistent damage removed without Elemental Reactions, the Burning status condition is removed, but Pyro remains for 2 rounds or until reacted with.
The damage increases by 1d4 per Elemental Mastery.

Catalyze

Catalyze is triggered when Dendro and Electro are applied to a target. The Catalyze status condition lasts for two rounds and functions as the Dendro status condition, and can trigger the same Elemental Reactions that Dendro is associated with, with the exception of Catalyze. As such, Catalyze and Electro can coexist without reacting.
A target with the Catalyze status condition takes 2 additional Dendro or Electro damage from each respective damage type. Combine the damage for the purposes of resistances and weaknesses.
The damage increases by 2 per Elemental Mastery.

Crystallize

Crystallize is triggered when Geo is applied to a target afflicted with Cryo, Electro, Pyro, or Wet. When Crystallize is triggered, an Elemental Shard spawns within 10 feet of the target in a space of the triggerer's choice. The Elemental Shard has a type matching that of the non-Geo element that triggered the reaction, and lasts for 3 rounds in its space before expiring.
If a player character or their ally enters or is in the space of the Elemental Shard when it spawns, it is consumed and they gain a Crystalline Shield, granting them 5 temporary Hit Points for 3 rounds, and 5 damage resistance to the element matching the type of the Elemental Shard as long as the temporary Hit Points are active. Multiple Crystalline Shields do not stack, and players only gain resistance matching the most recent Elemental Shard that was acquired.
The temporary Hit Points and damage resistance increase by 5 per Elemental Mastery.

Simultaneous Elemental Reactions

Sometimes, two or more elements will be applied to a creature simultaneously. This typically happens when elements that do not react with each other, e.g. Cryo and Dendro, or elements that do not immediately consume each other, e.g. Hydro and Electro, coexist on a target. In cases such as these, multiple Elemental Reactions can happen simultaneously. For example, if Pyro is applied to a creature in the above two examples, both Burning and Melt, or Vaporize and Overloaded will trigger, respectively.

Automatons

Automatons are powerful autonomous machines created by long gone civilizations. The majority of automatons are Ruin Machines, although automatons of different origin exist. They boast high attack power and physical resistances, but many have weak spots in exposed cores or specific vulnerabilities that can be exploited by canny adventurers.

Ruin Machines

Ruin Machines are ancient machines from the lost nation of Khaenri'ah and can be found across Teyvat, particularly in the ruins of ancient civilizations. Following the fall of Khaenri'ah, the Ruin Machines wandered aimlessly across Teyvat for hundreds of years, mostly ending up in the ruins of former civilizations, causing the people of the present era to mistake them as the guardians of the ruins, hence terming them "Ruin Machines". Some have completely broken down, while others remain active and roaming, or inactive until they sense people nearby.

Rule Changes

Alchemy

Alchemy is widely practiced across the seven nations of Teyvat for crafting, creating potions, enhancing gear, and transmuting materials. When alchemy harnesses the elements—the fundamental magical building blocks of Teyvat's world structure—it inherently becomes magical as well.
    Consumable alchemical items gain the Magical trait when they also have one the seven elemental traits. Players interested in crafting these items need only one of the Alchemical Crafting or Magical Crafting skill feats, not both.

Runes

Given the highly reactive nature of the elements in Teyvat, some runic items from Golarion may not function as expected in this world. For example, a runic +2 Striking Flaming Shocking longsword may function fine in Golarion, but in Teyvat the sword would be constantly triggering Overloaded reactions on itself and its surroundings, and be unable to function properly as a blade!
    In general, property runes that have traits that would cause Elemental Reactions with one another cannot be inscribed on the same weapon simultaneously. This is mainly to promote synergy between teammates and differing elements to trigger Elemental Reactions together, but also for balance purposes, seeing as Elemental Reactions are already a substantial increase in power for player characters in general.
